## Design Context

### Users
Account Managers (AMs) at iMPact Business Group, a staffing agency placing talent in engineering, IT, accounting, and business professional roles. There are 14 AMs who use this dashboard daily to review incoming job leads, contact hiring managers, and send personalized outreach. They need to move through leads quickly and efficiently while maintaining quality outreach.

### Brand Personality
**Professional. Energetic. Approachable.**

iMPact is a staffing agency that bridges companies with talent. The brand leans professional but injects warmth and energy through its signature amber/orange (#FFA000). The interface should feel like a sharp, well-built tool that AMs genuinely enjoy using, not a chore they endure.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode only. Deep navy backgrounds (#0F1E3D) with layered card surfaces (#1a2744). Glassmorphic accents with subtle backdrop blur and transparency.
- **Color system**: Navy (#1A4EA2) as primary accent, amber/orange (#FFA000 / #E8620A) as brand energy, teal (#6EE7C7) for success and active states, soft red (#ef6961) for destructive actions. Category-coded pills (teal, blue, amber, purple) for job types.
- **Typography**: Oswald for headings and labels (uppercase, tracked). Raleway for body text and inputs. Clean hierarchy through size, weight, and opacity rather than decoration.
- **Layout**: Card-based, single-column (920px max), generous spacing. Information density stays high but scannable. Flex-based responsive layout with no explicit breakpoints.
- **Icons**: Inline SVGs using currentColor for themability. No emoji as structural icons.
- **Motion**: Fast micro-interactions (150-200ms). Fade and slide for state changes. No decorative animation.
- **Anti-patterns**: No cluttered CRM feel. No generic enterprise gray. No walls of text. No em dashes or AI jargon anywhere in content.

### Design Principles
1. **Speed over ceremony** - AMs process many leads per session. Every interaction should be fast, obvious, and require minimal clicks. Prioritize scannability and quick action.
2. **Color with purpose** - Use the amber/orange brand energy to draw attention to what matters. Use teal for positive outcomes, red for caution. Never use color as decoration alone.
3. **Professional but alive** - The dark theme and clean typography keep it professional. Gradients, subtle glow shadows, and smooth transitions keep it feeling modern and engaging rather than sterile.
4. **Consistent patterns** - Every card, modal, button, and input follows the same visual language. New features inherit existing patterns rather than inventing new ones.
5. **Clarity over cleverness** - Labels are direct. Actions are obvious. No hidden functionality behind gestures or unmarked icons. If it does something, it says what it does.
